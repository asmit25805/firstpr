# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

This project exists specifically to help people make their first-ever open-source contribution.
That only works if the space is genuinely welcoming to people who are new, unsure of themselves,
or still learning the basics. We're committed to making participation in this project a
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, identity, or
any other personal characteristic.

## What we expect

- **Be patient, especially with beginners.** A question that seems obvious to you may not be
  obvious to someone on their first PR. Answer it kindly, or point to relevant docs — don't
  dismiss it.
- **Give feedback on the work, not the person.** "This function could be simplified" is useful.
  Comments about someone's competence, background, or right to be here are not welcome, ever.
- **Assume good faith.** Most confusion is confusion, not malice.
- **Disagree respectfully.** Technical disagreements are normal and healthy; personal attacks
  aren't.

## What's not okay

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, in any project space (issues, PRs, discussions, or
  anywhere else this project is discussed on the maintainers' behalf).
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, or deliberately derailing threads.
- Any conduct which would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This applies within all project spaces (the repository, its issues and pull requests, and any
official discussion channels), and also applies when someone is representing the project in
public spaces elsewhere.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by opening a private security
advisory on the repository, or by contacting the maintainers directly if that option is listed in
the repository. Reports will be reviewed and investigated as promptly as possible, with the
reporter's privacy respected throughout.

Maintainers who don't follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences
determined by the project's other maintainers.

Consequences for violations are decided at maintainer discretion and may include a warning,
temporary suspension from project spaces, or a permanent ban, depending on severity and history.
